Infrared sauna

What is an infrared sauna?

Infrared saunas (cabins) are used both in medical institutions, fitness centers, beauty salons, and at home. Infrared waves are emitted by any warm object, including the human body, and are absolutely safe for the body. In an infrared sauna, infrared radiation heats the body directly, and not air (heating the air takes no more than 20% of the energy released, whereas in a regular bath it takes up to 80%). As a result, the body experiences a more intense therapeutic effect than in a traditional sauna. Infrared waves warm tissues, organs, muscles, bones and joints, speed up the flow of blood and other body fluids.

Effect on the body

The therapeutic effects of infrared cabins are multifaceted. As a result of regular use of infrared procedures, the level of cholesterol in the blood decreases, which reduces the risk of cardiovascular diseases and stabilizes blood pressure. The walls of blood vessels become stronger and more elastic. The functioning of the immune system improves, The overall resistance of the body increases, and this in turn allows you to effectively counteract colds and flu.
