MKS - mobile drying complex

Wood drying technologies using infrared radiation were known more than half a century ago. The main obstacle to the widespread use of these technologies was high costs, since incandescent lamps with a power of 500 W and higher were used as infrared emitters. Research by scientists and practical application during drying have shown that radiant heat penetrates wood to a depth of 15 mm. In contrast to the convective method, the wood is heated, not the air, with the appearance of a self-moistening effect of the outer layers of wood. The self-moistening effect allows the technological process of removing hygroscopic moisture from solid wood without additional wetting of the agent. Specialists of Hit Life LLC created a product and technology for its use, allowing you to preserve the advantages of the method of drying wood using infrared radiation, developed back in the 30s of the last century, but significantly improve the results of the process. The improvement effect was achieved by using Heat Life film infrared heaters instead of incandescent lamps, cast iron stoves heated to red heat.

Drying technology

The heating element of the system is a film emitter “HeatLife”, which can heat up to +65°C. It is assembled into panels according to the size of the stack. An automatic process control system regulates the temperature depending on humidity and atmospheric pressure. Wood from 1 to 6 meters long and HeatLife panels are stacked with spacing on a reflective metallized film so that so that the thickness of the lumber adjacent to each panel does not exceed 250 mm. Automation sensors are installed and a vapor-permeable circuit is closed, isolating the stack from atmospheric air. The air inside the vapor-permeable circuit has higher temperature and humidity characteristics than atmospheric air. A vapor-permeable membrane allows moisture to pass out, but prevents moisture from entering from the outside. Isolation of the agent from the atmosphere allows you to create a greenhouse effect and maintain a high temperature of the agent, which has a beneficial effect on the drying agent and allows you to increase its moisture capacity from 30 to 130 grams per cubic meter. The peculiarity of the method is that it is not the drying agent that heats the wood, but, on the contrary, the wood heats the drying agent and increases its moisture capacity.
